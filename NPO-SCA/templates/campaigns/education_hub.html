Education Hub

Building mental health literacy through engaging, accessible content.

Knowledge is power. Our Education Hub provides weekly doses of mental health information, delivered in digestible formats that fit into a student's busy life. From monthly deep-dives to quick trivia, we cover the facts, bust the myths, and arm students with knowledge they can use to support themselves and others.

Our educational content is developed with guidance from our Mental Health Consultant and draws from authoritative sources including SAMHSA's Risk and Protective Factors resources, the Surgeon General's Advisory on Protecting Youth Mental Health, the Jed Foundation, and Mental Health.gov. We translate clinical information into language that's accessible to teens while maintaining accuracy and evidence-based foundations.

Echo of the Month

Monthly Mental Health Spotlight

Each month, we spotlight a specific mental health topic with detailed education, including what it is, how it affects people, common misconceptions, and support strategies. Our goal is to build a knowledgeable community armed with actionable information for students and allies alike.

  • September: Social Isolation - We launched our campaign by educating our community about social isolation, its prevalence among youth, and our risk and protective factors framework.
  • October: Social Anxiety Disorder (SAD) - We explored social anxiety as more than just shyness, explaining how it can interfere with daily activities.
  • November: Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD) - We examined chronic worry and its physical and emotional effects.
  • December: Seasonal Affective Disorder - We provided education about seasonal depression and light therapy.
  • January: New Beginnings and Goal-Setting - Guidance on setting realistic intentions without perfectionism.
  • February: Black History Month Mental Health - Mental health resources and perspectives from Black communities.

Autism Awareness

"Different, Not Less"

A core part of our mission is fighting social isolation for neurodivergent students. This series focuses specifically on Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), debunking myths and offering guidance on how to build inclusive friendships and communities.

We explore how sensory processing and social communication differences impact mental health, fostering a culture where neurodiversity is celebrated rather than stigmatized. Content includes how to be a better ally to autistic peers, understanding sensory needs, and creating inclusive social environments.

Our partnership with Gifted People Services ensures this content reflects the perspectives and needs of the autism community.

Monthly Trivia and Echo Bites

"Fast Facts for Busy Minds"

Learning doesn't have to be heavy. Monthly Trivia and Echo Bites offer quick, engaging ways to test your knowledge and learn new facts. From True/False quizzes about anxiety prevalence to bite-sized psychology tips, these posts make mental health literacy fun, interactive, and easy to digest while scrolling.

Our interactive format encourages engagement and makes learning feel like a game rather than a lecture. Studies show that testing yourself on information improves retention, so our trivia format actually helps students remember what they learn.
