Education Hub

Deep dives into mental health topics, facts, and awareness.

Understanding is the first step toward empathy. Our Education Hub is designed to demystify mental health, break down complex disorders, and provide accessible, researched-backed information. We believe that when students understand why they feel the way they do, and what their peers are experiencing, we create a more inclusive and compassionate school culture.

Echo of the Month

"Shining a Light on the Unseen"

Each month, E.C.H.O.E. dedicates its platform to a specific mental health topic. From Social Anxiety Disorder (SAD) in October to Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD) in November, we provide comprehensive resources, symptom breakdowns, and support strategies.

Our goal is to normalize these conversations and provide clear, actionable information for students and allies alike.

Autism Awareness

"Different, Not Less"

A core part of our mission is fighting social isolation for neurodivergent students. This series focuses specifically on Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), debunking myths and offering guidance on how to build inclusive friendships.

We explore how sensory processing and social communication differences impact mental health, fostering a culture where neurodiversity is celebrated.

Monthly Trivia & Echo Bites

"Fast Facts for Busy Minds"

Learning doesn't have to be heavy. Monthly Trivia and Echo Bites offer quick, engaging ways to test your knowledge and learn new facts.

From True/False quizzes about anxiety prevalence to bite-sized psychology tips, these posts make mental health literacy fun, interactive, and easy to digest while scrolling.
