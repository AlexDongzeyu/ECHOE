Student Voices

Real stories from real students - because connection is protection.

We believe in the power of lived experience to create connection. This section features the voices and experiences of students in our community - sharing their struggles, their triumphs, and their unfiltered thoughts. By showcasing real stories, we remind every student that while their journey is unique, they are never walking it alone.

Research consistently shows that knowing others share similar experiences reduces feelings of isolation and normalizes help-seeking behavior. When students see their peers talking openly about mental health challenges, they're more likely to recognize their own struggles as valid and reach out for support. Our Student Voices content creates this crucial sense of shared experience.

The Letter Contest

"Let Your Voice Echo"

Words have the power to heal. Our Letter Writing Contest invites students to pen anonymous letters - to themselves, to a friend, to a stranger who needs hope, or to their community. Writing allows for reflection and processing that may not happen in everyday conversation.

Letters can address any aspect of mental health: what you've learned from difficult experiences, what you wish someone had told you, what gives you hope, or what you want others to know about struggles they may not see. The most meaningful entries receive special E.C.H.O.E. mental wellness prize boxes, and selected letters may be shared (anonymously) to inspire others and demonstrate the power of authentic expression.
