Student Voices

Real stories from real students — because connection is protection.

We believe in the power of lived experience to create connection. This section features the voices and experiences of students in our community — sharing their struggles, their triumphs, and their unfiltered thoughts. By showcasing real stories, we remind every student that while their journey is unique, they are never walking it alone.

Research consistently shows that knowing others share similar experiences reduces feelings of isolation and normalizes help-seeking behavior. When students see their peers talking openly about mental health challenges, they're more likely to recognize their own struggles as valid and reach out for support. Our Student Voices content creates this crucial sense of shared experience.

Echo Voices

"Connection is Protection"

Every face in the hallway has a story you haven't heard. Echo Voices is our signature interview series featuring students from different grades and backgrounds. We ask the real questions: When do you feel most alone? What gives you hope? How do you cope when things get hard?

Through honest portraits and candid interviews, these stories serve as our strongest protection against isolation. Participants share what mental health means to them, moments when they felt most connected or disconnected, and advice for others who might be struggling. The diversity of voices — representing different experiences, challenges, and coping strategies — ensures that every student can find someone whose story resonates with their own.

Featured students have shared experiences ranging from navigating social anxiety to processing family challenges, from the isolation of being an international student to the pressure of academic expectations. Each story is a reminder that vulnerability creates connection, and that speaking our truth can help others feel less alone.

Echo Gallery

"When Feelings Speak"

Sometimes, emotions are too complex for words. The Echo Gallery showcases creative submissions from our community — art, photography, poetry, and visual expressions of mental health. It is a safe space where creativity meets vulnerability, allowing students to see their internal worlds reflected in the art of others.

Art has long been recognized as a powerful tool for emotional expression and processing. For students who struggle to articulate their feelings verbally, creative expression offers an alternative pathway to be heard and understood. The Echo Gallery celebrates these expressions and creates opportunities for connection through shared aesthetic experiences.

We accept submissions on an ongoing basis and feature selected works across our social media platforms and website. All submissions are shared with artist permission, and participants can choose to remain anonymous or be credited for their work.

The Letter Contest

"Let Your Voice Echo"

Words have the power to heal. Our Letter Writing Contest invites students to pen anonymous letters — to themselves, to a friend, to a stranger who needs hope, or to their community. Writing allows for reflection and processing that may not happen in everyday conversation.

Letters can address any aspect of mental health: what you've learned from difficult experiences, what you wish someone had told you, what gives you hope, or what you want others to know about struggles they may not see. The most meaningful entries receive special E.C.H.O.E. mental wellness prize boxes, and selected letters may be shared (anonymously) to inspire others and demonstrate the power of authentic expression.
