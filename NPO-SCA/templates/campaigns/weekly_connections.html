Weekly Connections

Feel-Good Fridays and seasonal celebrations.

Connection doesn't always require a grand gesture; often, it's built in the small, consistent moments. This section houses our recurring initiatives designed to spark joy and foster routine interaction with our community. Whether it's a weekly challenge or a holiday check-in, these campaigns encourage students to step out of their comfort zones and engage with the people around them.

Consistency is key to building protective factors. By showing up weekly with supportive content, we become a reliable presence in our followers' lives - a reminder that someone is thinking about their wellbeing and offering actionable ways to feel a little better.

Feel-Good Friday

"Small Acts, Big Impact"

Every Friday, we issue a simple, actionable challenge to the student body. Whether it's "Say hi to someone new," "Go tech-free for an hour," or "Send a gratitude text," these micro-challenges are designed to reduce social anxiety and build habit-forming connections. Each challenge is small enough to feel achievable but meaningful enough to create real impact.

It's our way of turning the weekend into an opportunity for wellness. Research shows that small positive actions can compound over time, creating upward spirals of wellbeing. By making these actions feel fun and achievable, we help students build protective habits without overwhelming them.

Past challenges have included: complimenting someone genuinely, spending 10 minutes outside, reaching out to someone you haven't talked to in a while, writing down three things you're grateful for, and doing one kind thing for yourself.

Seasonal Campaigns

"Holiday Mental Health Checks"

Holidays can be joyful, but they can also be isolating. Our seasonal campaigns provide tailored advice for navigating the highs and lows of the school year. We focus on inclusive ways to celebrate that prioritize mental well-being over social pressure.

  • "Halloween with Heart" - We offered SAD-friendly activities for students who find Halloween overwhelming, along with tips for managing social anxiety around costumes and parties.
  • "Gratitude in Action" - For Thanksgiving, we moved beyond generic gratitude posts to practical ways of expressing appreciation and connecting with family during potentially stressful gatherings.
  • Winter Break Check-ins and Connection Ideas - We provided content to help students manage the transition away from school routines, maintain connections during break, and cope with family stress or loneliness during the holidays.
