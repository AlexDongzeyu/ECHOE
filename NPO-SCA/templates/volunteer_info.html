Become a Volunteer

Join our mission to bring light to those in silence

Why Volunteer With Us?

At Light in Silence, volunteers are the heart of our mission. Your compassionate responses can provide hope, comfort, and connection to someone who needs it most. Every letter you respond to has the potential to transform a life.

Make a Real Impact

Your words can be the light someone needs in their darkest moment. Provide hope and support when it matters most.

Learn & Grow

Develop empathy, communication skills, and gain valuable experience in mental health support through our comprehensive training.

Join a Community

Connect with like-minded individuals who share your passion for mental health advocacy and community support.

Volunteer Roles

Letter Responder

  • Read and respond to anonymous letters
  • Provide empathetic, supportive responses
  • Work with our moderation team
  • 5-10 hours per week commitment

Perfect for those who love writing and want to provide direct support.

Mailbox Coordinator

  • Manage physical mailbox locations
  • Collect and digitize physical letters
  • Coordinate with local communities
  • Flexible schedule based on location

Ideal for those who enjoy community engagement and logistical coordination.

Community Moderator

  • Monitor community chat spaces
  • Ensure safe, supportive environment
  • Facilitate group discussions
  • Evening and weekend hours

Great for those experienced in online community management.

Event Coordinator

  • Plan and organize community events
  • Coordinate workshops and seminars
  • Manage event logistics and promotion
  • Project-based commitment

Perfect for organized individuals with event planning experience.

What We're Looking For

Essential Qualities

  • Empathy and compassion
  • Strong communication skills
  • Commitment to confidentiality
  • Non-judgmental attitude
  • Reliable and dependable

Requirements

  • 18 years of age or older
  • Canadian resident
  • Background check clearance
  • Complete training program
  • Minimum 3-month commitment

Training & Support

We provide comprehensive training to ensure you feel confident and prepared in your volunteer role.

Mental Health Awareness

Understanding mental health conditions, crisis recognition, and appropriate responses.

Communication Skills

Learn effective listening, empathetic responding, and appropriate boundaries.

Safety & Ethics

Confidentiality protocols, mandatory reporting, and self-care practices.

Ongoing Support

Regular supervision, peer support groups, and continued learning opportunities.
